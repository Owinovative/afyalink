# Visual Rejection Audit

Milestone 6 treats the previous frontend direction as rejected. The issue was not only spacing or color; the public site still read like a developer-built product surface with marketing copy. This audit records the visible failure points and the corrective direction.

## Public Routes

| Route | What looked weak | Required rebuild |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `/` | The homepage relied too heavily on repeated cards, small centered compositions, and software-style panels. It did not create an immediate premium healthcare impression. | Rebuild as a photo-led editorial homepage with a near-full first view, human healthcare imagery, three-audience story, trust workflow, placement story, and restrained CTA rhythm. |
| `/professionals` | The page explained workflow but did not feel aspirational or respectful enough for licensed professionals. Copy carried too much of the weight. | Lead with professional imagery, short copy, and a practical path from profile to publication and placement preferences. |
| `/students` | The student track was functionally clear but visually felt like an add-on. It needed stronger identity and a safer explanation of waiting-license status. | Give students their own warm page with training imagery, clear “start early” promise, and firm “not facility-publishable yet” boundaries. |
| `/facilities` | The facility page lacked executive presence and did not make facility demand, requisitions, access, and shortlists feel commercially serious. | Use facility/team photography, compact access explanation, marketplace context, and placement-grade calls to action. |
| `/matching` | The matching story could look abstract and technical instead of operationally trustworthy. | Make matching feel explainable, human-reviewed, and placement-focused, with photo-supported sections and no AI hype. |
| `/trust-security` | Security content risked becoming a dense compliance text page. | Use architecture-style grouping with real secure-review photography, concise bullets, and explicit screenshot/watermark/audit language. |
| `/verification` | Verification could look like another generic process grid. | Present credential review, regulatory checks, interview scoring, qualification, and publication as a controlled assessment rail. |
| `/pricing-access` | The page could imply pricing clarity that is not finalized, or feel too thin. | Keep it honest: approved access, entitlement, payment references, M-PESA readiness, and contact-driven commercial next steps. |
| `/about` | The mission page was too operational and not memorable enough. | Reframe around healthcare trust infrastructure and why verified placement matters. |
| `/contact` | Contact copy was useful but not polished. The form area needed stronger visual context and less generic structure. | Use compact split layout, contact purposes, and a reminder that private documents belong in authenticated portals. |
| `/faq` | Long lists could become visually flat. | Group questions by role and keep answers short, scannable, and policy-accurate. |

## Auth Routes

| Route | What looked weak | Required rebuild |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `/auth/login` | Auth pages could feel generic compared with the public site. | Keep compact split layouts, refined form cards, and role-aware copy. |
| `/auth/register/professional` | Professional registration needed stronger distinction from student/facility paths. | Maintain clear licensed-applicant language and avoid overpromising publication. |
| `/auth/register/student` | Student registration must not look like a reduced professional registration page. | Explain that license is pending and facility publication remains locked until conversion. |
| `/auth/register/facility` | Facility registration needed stronger organization onboarding framing. | Emphasize legal organization review, controlled access, and admin approval. |

## Portal Areas

| Area | What looked weak | Required rebuild |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Professional dashboard | Status cards could feel scattered and visually cheap. | Use a calmer portal shell, compact panels, stronger progress hierarchy, and clearer next actions. |
| Student waiting-license dashboard | Waiting-license status needed stronger visual priority. | Make track identity visible and explain what is complete, missing, and locked. |
| Facility dashboard | Facility access, recommendations, requisitions, and candidate browsing needed better grouping. | Present facility operations as an account console, not a marketing page. |
| Admin dashboard | Admin surfaces must remain dense but readable. | Improve spacing, panel hierarchy, table rhythm, and counters without turning operations into oversized cards. |

## Cross-Cutting Failures

- Too many similarly weighted cards weakened hierarchy.
- Some sections were either too sparse on desktop or too repetitive.
- Photography existed but was not central enough to the brand language.
- Header and footer felt functional, not premium.
- Copy sometimes explained backend features instead of telling a crisp product story.
- Portal surfaces lacked enough visual discipline to match the public promise.

## Milestone 6 Correction

The new direction is a premium healthcare trust platform with real human photography, strong editorial layout, calm authority, and operational clarity. Public pages should feel like a serious healthcare company. Portals should feel like practical operating software. Backend rules remain authoritative; the frontend’s role is to guide and present workflow state clearly.
